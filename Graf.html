Graf

18 Februari 2025 oleh Riski

If we imagine a network of MRT (Mass Rapid Transit) stations, we can represent those stations as points and the routes connecting two stations as lines. The network of points and lines is what we call a graph.

A graph consists of a set of points called vertices and a set of lines connecting two different vertices, called edges. A graph is usually denoted by $G = (V, E)$, where $V$ is the set of vertices and $E$ is the set of edges.

At the beginning of the birth of graph theory, there was a problem that was very difficult to solve at that time. In a city called Königsberg (now Kaliningrad, Russia), there were seven bridges in a river area. The problem was whether someone could cross all the bridges from a point and end at the same point without crossing any bridge more than once? In 1735, a great mathematician of that time tried to solve the problem.

He tried to transform the bridges into edges and the endpoints of the bridges into vertices. Thus, the image would become a graph and he analyzed it mathematically. He concluded that it was impossible to do so. This was the beginning of graph theory, which is a part of combinatorial mathematics.

Graph theory is widely used in mathematics and other fields. It can be used in network theory, finding the shortest route for a journey, scheduling, and analyzing the structure of a chemical molecule. The development of graph theory has been very rapid. Recently, many mathematicians have conducted research in the field of topological graph theory and have devoted their energy to analyzing the properties of chemical molecules. Mathematicians also relate abstract algebraic structures to graphs to visualize them in a form that is easier to imagine.
